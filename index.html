    Central Tendency (Median): The median weight loss for Diet A (5.642 kg) is substantially higher than for Diet B (3.745 kg).
    This confirms the conclusion from the mean in Exercise 6.1: Diet A is more effective.
    The median tells us that the "typical" or "middle-of-the-road" person on Diet A lost nearly 2 kg more than their counterpart on Diet B.

    Spread/Variability (IQR): The Interquartile Range (IQR) for Diet B (3.451 kg) is slightly larger than for Diet A (3.285 kg).
    This means that the middle 50% of weight loss results for Diet B are more spread out.
    In other words, there was more inconsistency in the amount of weight lost by the central group of participants on Diet B compared to Diet A.
